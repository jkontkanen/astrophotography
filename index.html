Astrophotography by Janne Kontkanen

This page documents my astrophotography journey. Like many others I began by attempting to take Milky Way photos with my cellphone. I was first inspired by the Pixel phone photos of Florian Kainz with whom I used to work with at Google. That we got into RV camping to fight the boredom of the pandemic in 2020 definitely helped by providing almost unlimited access to starry skies. While cell phone is a great entry point to this hobby, I continued via DSLR and lightweight star trackers to computerized mounts, dedicated telescopes, astrocameras and many trips to dark skies far away from light pollution.

Why I do this, I am still not 100% sure. It is certainly a time-sink and you can hardly dream of competing with James Webb and Hubble space telescopes. Perhaps one day I'll discover my own nebula or something. Or contribute to the field with an algorithm. The latter is probably more likely given my life-long background in computer graphics, vision and machine learning research. However, I still consider myself a beginner. This hobby has a very steep learning curve. So at this point rather I prefer to say I do this just because I like it.

The reason I made a dedicated web-page is that I have not identified a photo sharing platform that supports HDR-images and displays the content the way I like it. HDR & deep sky astrophotography is not a common thing to see, so here you have it (assuming you have access to Chrome browser and HDR-capable display).
